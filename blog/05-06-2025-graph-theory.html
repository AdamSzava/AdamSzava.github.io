Trees have n-1 Edges

Posted on: 05-06-2025

Proposition: For a tree $T$ of order $n\geq 1$, $T$ contains $n-1$ edges.

Proof:

We proceed by induction on $n$, the number of nodes.

For $n=1$, $T \simeq K_1$, which has $0 = 1-1$ edges.

Suppose all trees of order $k \geq 1$ have $k-1$ edges. Take an arbitrary tree $T$ of order $k+1$, we must show it has $k$ edges. $T$ must contain a leaf $v$ (by yesterday's proposition, all trees of order $n \geq 2$ have leaves, and $T$ is of order at least $2$).

Consider the graph $T' = T-v$, $T'$ is a tree (since a leaf is never a cut vertex (?)) and is of order $k$, therefore we know by the inductive hypothesis that $T'$ has $k-1$ edges. $T$ can be constructed from $T-v$ by adding a single edge and vertex, and so the number of edges in $T$ is $$\#E(T) = \#E(T-v) +1 =k-1+1 = k$$ $\blacksquare$
