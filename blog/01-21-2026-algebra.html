Elementary Exercises Relating to Groups

Posted on: 01-21-2026

The following exercises appears in Judson's Abstract Algebra with Applications as exercise 25. Note that I will use multiplicative notation for the group operation, and the group identity is denoted as $e$.
Proposition: Let $(G,\cdot)$ be a group, and $a,b\in G$. Show that $\forall n\in \mathbb{Z}, ab^{n}a^{-1} = (aba^{-1})^n$.

Proof:

We proceed by induction on $n\geq 0$ then we extend this to all of $\mathbb{Z}$. Let $n = 0$, and note that $a^0 := e$ for any $a\in G$. Then $ab^0a^{-1} = aea^{-1} = aa^{-1} = e = (aba^{-1})^0$. Also, consider $n=1$, $ab^1a^{-1} = aba^{-1} = (aba^{-1})^1$ (technically, should the base case be $n = 0$ or $n= 1$?).

Now let $k > 1$. Suppose $ab^ka^{-1} = (aba^{-1})^k$. Now consider $(aba^{-1})^{k+1} = (aba^{-1})(aba^{-1})^k = (aba^{-1})(ab^ka^{-1}) = aba^{-1}ab^ka^{-1} = abeb^ka^{-1} = abb^ka^{-1} = ab^{k+1}a^{-1}$
